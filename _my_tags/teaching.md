# Built environment

## GECH615/780 - Physique du bâtiment : Éclairage

<a id="external" href="https://www.univ-smb.fr/polytech/formation/batiment-ecoconstruction-energie/" target="_blank">*Cycle Ingénieur Bâtiment Écoconstruction Énergie (BAT)*</a><br>
*Filière Classique (FISE - Licence 3), Filière Alternance (FISA - Master 1)*<br>
*Polytech Annecy-Chambéry*

**Content ~** Main principles and fundamental concepts of lighting (definitions and concerns, light and the human visual system, photometric quantities and units, surface properties); natural light (environmental concerns and its impact on human beings, definition of the solar resource and influence of the atmosphere and urban fabric on its distribution, criteria and metrics for the daylight provision in buildings, control systems); artificial lighting (key figures and objectives, production of light, properties and examples of common artificial light sources, role and features of lamps, norms and regulations).
The course is taught in French.

# Radiative transfer

## ENER751 - Introduction to radiative transfer

<a id="external" href="https://www.univ-smb.fr/solaracademy/master-energy-for-solar-buildings-and-cities-esbc-program-2/" target="_blank">*Master 1 Energy for Solar Buildings and Cities (ESBC)*</a><br>
*Solar Academy - Université Savoie Mont Blanc*

**Content ~** Introduction to radiative transfer including electromagnetic wave theory, main radiative quantities, blackbody emission, radiative and optical properties (of solids, gases, particles), measurement and instrumentation techniques; Exchanges between diffuse surfaces including net fluxes definition and view factors concept.
Lecture slides are courtesy of Cyril Caliot (LMAP - UPPA). 
The course is taught in English.

## ENER858 - Advanced radiation modelling in complex media

<a id="external" href="https://www.univ-smb.fr/solaracademy/master-energy-for-solar-buildings-and-cities-esbc-program-2/" target="_blank">*Master 1 Energy for Solar Buildings and Cities (ESBC)*</a><br>
*Solar Academy - Université Savoie Mont Blanc*

**Content ~** Introduction to the UNIX environment and to the RADIANCE rendering engine (installation, basic command lines); Influence of optical and radiative properties of materials on the distribution of incident radiations; Distinction between natural and artificial light sources; Simulation and analysis of visible radiations emitted in a given scene of simple configuration (creation and arrangement of the geometries, definition of surface materials properties, compilation of the overall setup and modelling of radiations, visualisation and interpretation of the rendering).
The course is taught in English.
