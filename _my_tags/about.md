## Academic background and working experience

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher in urban solar radiation modelling and analysis at <a id="external" href="https://www.oie.minesparis.psl.eu/Accueil/" target="_blank">Centre O.I.E.</a>, Mines Paris - PSL.

I conducted my Ph.D. at <a id="external" href="https://www.univ-smb.fr/locie/" target="_blank">LOCIE</a> (UMR CNRS 5271), Université Savoie Mont Blanc under the supervision of Julien Ramousse and Martin Thebault, and defended my work in December 2025.
This research has aimed at better understanding and anticipating influence of the urban system on the solar resource distribution in space and time using modal decomposition methods.
During my doctoral studies, I had the opportunity to visit the <a id="external" href="https://math.unibo.it/en" target="_blank">Department of Mathematics</a> from the University of Bologna for a couple of months, to work on higher-order tensor factorisations in the team of Valeria Simoncini.

Prior to my Ph.D., I completed an engineering degree (Dipl.-Ing.) in optics and applied mathematics from <a id="external" href="https://www.institutoptique.fr/" target="_blank">Institut d’Optique</a>, Université Paris-Saclay.
In my second year, I conducted a four months research internship at <a id="external" href="https://www.entpe.fr/laboratoire-de-tribologie-et-de-dynamique-des-systemes-ltds" target="_blank">LTDS</a> (UMR ENTPE-CNRS 5513) to work on hyperspectral data acquisition, processing and analysis for improving the visual perception of digitally-visualised lit urban scenes (supervision: Raphaël Labayrade).
Building upon this experience, I eventually decided in my final year at Institut d'Optique to enroll in a dual M.Sc. degree with the <a id="external" href="https://www.ucl.ac.uk/bartlett/environment-energy-resources" target="_blank">Bartlett School of Environment, Energy and Resources</a>, University College London (UCL).
There, I gained additional expertise about various aspects of the solar resource in the built environment.

Besides, I have also been involved in consultancy and research projects in the broad area of (day)lighting for private clients and public institutions, working as a lighting consultant for <a id="external" href="https://bregroup.com/" target="_blank">BRE Group</a> in the team of Cosmin Ticleanu.

For additional details about my background, teaching experience and research dissemination please see my <a id="external" href="{{ site.files.cv | relative_url }}" target="_blank">**curriculum vitae**</a> (English version, updated June 2026) or visit relevant pages on this website.

## Current research interests 

Today, my research interests span across solar energy, urban radiative transfer, Monte Carlo modelling and data-driven statistical analysis methods.
I am especially interested in improving the efficiency-accuracy trade-off of radiative transfer modelling on the urban scale leveraging physics-informed statistics.
